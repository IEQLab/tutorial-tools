Room Acoustics Calculator

Three routine acoustic calculations for the Week 6 tutorial: correctly averaging or combining sound levels in decibels, and the Sabine relationship between room volume, absorption, and reverberation time. It does the arithmetic; benchmarking against AS/NZS 2107 and the design interpretation are yours.

1. Average or combine sound levels (dB)

Decibels are logarithmic, so you cannot take a plain arithmetic mean. Enter your levels (dB), separated by commas or spaces.

Average: L = 10 · log₁₀( (1/n) · Σ 10^(Li/10) ) · Combine: L = 10 · log₁₀( Σ 10^(Li/10) )

Use the energy average to summarise repeated LAeq or octave-band readings of the same source. Use combine when adding independent sources together.

2. Sabine: absorption and reverberation time

The Sabine equation links room volume (V), total absorption (A, in m² sabine), and reverberation time (T, in seconds). Enter volume plus one of T or A; the other is computed.

A = 0.16 · V / T · T = 0.16 · V / A

3. Speech intelligibility — quick estimate

A rough Speech Transmission Index from the speech-to-noise ratio. Useful for a first read on whether speech is easy or hard to follow.

SNR = Lspeech − Lnoise · STI ≈ (SNR + 15) / 30 (clamped 0–1)
Indicative only. This SNR shortcut ignores reverberation and per-band effects. For anything you report, use the class STI spreadsheet or the full IEC 60268-16 method.

Qualitative bands (IEC 60268-16): <0.30 bad · 0.30–0.45 poor · 0.45–0.60 fair · 0.60–0.75 good · >0.75 excellent.

Sabine equation and logarithmic level arithmetic are exact; the STI figure is a speech-to-noise approximation for teaching only. Teaching aid for BPSD5030; benchmark results against AS/NZS 2107:2016 and confirm against measurement software.
