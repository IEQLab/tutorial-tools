IAQ & Ventilation Calculator

Work out the required outdoor airflow for a space (ASHRAE 62.1), then estimate the actual ventilation from measured CO2 using a steady-state mass balance. The tool does the arithmetic; the interpretation (is the space adequately ventilated, and what would you change) is yours.

1. Required outdoor airflow

ASHRAE 62.1 Ventilation Rate Procedure. Pick a space type to load typical Rp/Ra, or enter your own from Table 6.2.2.1.

Vbz = (Rp × Pz) + (Ra × Az) · Voz = Vbz / Ez

Ez = 1.0 assumes fully mixed air. Real systems are often 0.5–0.8; lower effectiveness means more airflow is needed for the same air quality.

2. Actual ventilation from measured CO2

Steady-state mass balance: when indoor CO2 stops rising, generation and removal are balanced, so you can back-calculate the outdoor airflow. Read the steady-state CO2 off your measured curve (where it flattens).

Qperson = G / (Ci − Co) · Qtotal = Qperson × N · ACH = (Qtotal × 3.6) / Volume

CO2 in ppm is converted to a fraction internally (ppm ÷ 1,000,000). G defaults to 0.005 L/s for an adult at rest; adjust for activity or age.

3. Occupancy scenario explorer

Holding the ventilation rate (Qtotal) from section 2 fixed, see how steady-state CO2 would shift as occupancy changes. The dashed line is the ASHRAE 1,000 ppm guideline.

Ci = Co + ((N × G) / Qtotal) × 10⁶
Formulas: ASHRAE 62.1 Ventilation Rate Procedure and a steady-state single-zone CO2 mass balance. This is a teaching aid for BPSD5030. Always sanity-check the output against your own data and the source standard.
