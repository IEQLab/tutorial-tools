Game the Rating

You are the IEQ consultant. One building, two rating systems, a fixed budget. Hit the target, then look at how much of your rating was real improvement and how much was clever measurement. NABERS rates measured performance, so you game the measurement. WELL counts credits, so you game the selection. Both let the badge run ahead of what occupants actually feel.

Act 1

NABERS IE Star Challenge

Five dimensions, each rated 1 to 6 stars from its measured performance; the overall star is their average (NABERS weights all five equally). Spend your budget on real upgrades (they lift what occupants experience and what the sensors read) or on cheaper measurement tactics (they lift only what the sensors read). Reach the target star without going over budget.

The maths behind the star

Each dimension carries a true score (0 to 100, what occupants experience) and a measured score (what the rating sees). A real upgrade adds to both. A measurement tactic adds only to the measured score, because it changes when, where, or how you sample, not the building.

measured = clamp(true + tactic_inflation, 0, 100) dimension star = clamp(1 + measured / 20, 1, 6) overall star = mean of the five dimension stars

Real thresholds sit behind this (thermal = % time operative temperature inside the ASHRAE 55 band, roughly 75% for 4 stars and 85%+ for 5; IAQ = % time CO2 at or below 1000 ppm; acoustics at or below 55 dB(A) open-plan; lighting 300 to 500 lux with glare control; layout by m2/person and satisfaction). The 0 to 100 scale is a teaching simplification of those bands, not the NABERS algorithm.

Act 2

WELL Credit Round

Same building, a different logic. WELL v2 awards points for optional features across its concepts; hit a points threshold and you certify. Preconditions (particulate limits, a ventilation design, no indoor smoking, acoustic zoning, a mental-health commitment) are assumed met. Now buy optional credits with your effort budget to reach the target band. Watch which credits actually change the indoor environment and which just need a policy or a form.

How the points and bands work here

The real WELL v2 standard certifies at 40 (Bronze), 50 (Silver), 60 (Gold), and 80 (Platinum) points out of about 100. This round models a representative slice of the health-relevant concepts (Air, Light, Thermal, Sound, Mind) worth points, so the band lines are scaled to that slice by the same 40/50/60/80 percent proportions.

Point values are the real per-feature maximums from the WELL v2 scorecard (Q3 to Q4 2024). The effort costs and the occupant-benefit rating (three dots = high, two = partial, one = policy or awareness only) are teaching estimates, set to expose the gap between what scores points and what improves the space.

A teaching aid for BPSD5030 Week 9. NABERS bands and dimensions follow the Week 9 lecture; WELL point values are from the public WELL v2 scorecard (Q3 to Q4 2024). The scoring is deliberately simplified to make the gaming logic visible in a tutorial. It is not the NABERS or WELL algorithm; do not use its numbers in an assignment.
