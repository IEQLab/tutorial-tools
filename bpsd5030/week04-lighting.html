Lighting Uniformity Explorer

Enter the illuminance readings you took across a working plane, laid out as a grid in the positions you measured them. This maps the light spatially so you can see the unevenness that a single average lux value hides, and it reports the uniformity ratio you compute for Assignment 1. It does the arithmetic and the picture; the figure you submit, and the reading of what it means, stay yours.

1. Your measurement grid

Set the grid to match how you walked the space (e.g. 4 columns across the desk, 4 rows front-to-back), then type each reading in lux into the cell at that position. Leave a cell blank where you did not take a reading; blanks are ignored in the maths and shown empty on the map.

Values are in lux. Grid positions are relative, so orient it however you measured (e.g. top row nearest the window).

2. Spatial map

Each cell is shaded by its reading (dark = low lux, bright = high lux). The lowest and highest readings are ringed. This is what the mean cannot show you: where the light falls away.

3. Uniformity

How these relate to the lighting standards (EN 12464-1)

The uniformity ratio here, U0 = minimum ÷ average, is the same quantity EN 12464-1 uses to specify indoor lighting. That standard sets a minimum U0 on a defined task-area grid: commonly U0 ≥ 0.60 for the task area itself (e.g. a desk or workbench) and 0.40 for the immediate surround. AS/NZS 1680 works from the same idea. Two cautions before you quote a number: the standards prescribe a grid spacing and a task area (roughly square cells, sized to the space), so a comparable U0 needs a reasonably regular grid over the task plane, not a handful of scattered points; and diversity (minimum ÷ maximum) is a different, stricter ratio some guides also quote, so name which one you are reporting.

4. Spread of readings

The same readings as a box plot: the box spans the middle half (25th to 75th percentile), the line is the median, the whiskers reach the minimum and maximum, and each dot is one reading.

Take-home reminder: this view is for understanding the spread in class. The figure you submit for the analytical thread must be one you build (labelled axes with units, a legend where needed, and a caption that tells the reader what to notice), not a screenshot of this tool. Building it is the A2 Results skill being marked.

Uniformity ratio U0 = minimum ÷ average illuminance; diversity = minimum ÷ maximum. Statistics are computed over the cells you filled in. Teaching aid for BPSD5030; a comparable, standards-quotable uniformity figure needs a regular measurement grid over the task area (see the EN 12464-1 note). Validate against the source standard before reporting.
